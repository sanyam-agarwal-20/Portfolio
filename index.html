Prayagraj experiences frequent flooding from the Ganga and Yamuna rivers, especially during the monsoon. After floodwaters recede, the city faces a secondary crisis: Waterborne diseases like dengue, malaria, and cholera spread quickly due to stagnant water. Snake infestations and decomposing corpses pose serious public health and safety hazards. Poor tracking of affected households delays relief distribution, health interventions, and sanitation drives. The lack of real-time data, monitoring, and coordination worsens the problem, leaving many vulnerable populations underserved.

Flood Aftermath Response & Monitoring System (FARMS)

Following the floods in Prayagraj, stagnant water, unhygienic conditions, and delayed response efforts often contribute to the outbreak of diseases, snake infestations, and broader public health emergencies. To effectively tackle these challenges, a tech-driven solution can be developed that maps affected areas, crowdsources hazard reports from citizens, and enables authorities to coordinate more swiftly and efficiently. The system would feature a React.js-based web dashboard for administrators to monitor situations in real-time. On the backend, Node.js would provide REST APIs, with PostgreSQL or Firebase used for storing and retrieving real-time data. AI and machine learning would enhance the platform through image classification—detecting waterlogging or waste from satellite or drone images—and natural language processing (NLP) to analyze citizen-submitted reports for urgency or health-related symptoms. For spatial analysis, geotagged data visualization would be powered by Google Maps API, along with satellite data integration from sources like Sentinel Hub or ISRO Bhuvan. Additionally, to ensure accessibility in low-connectivity areas, the system would support SMS-based hazard reporting via services like Twilio and include local database caching for offline usage.
